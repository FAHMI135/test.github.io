Types of Logistics Services and Delivery Range

Logistics is the backbone of the modern supply chain, ensuring goods arrive on time and in good condition to customers.

In the world of logistics, there are various types of services that cover different shipping needs. The following are several types of logistics services that are commonly used:

1. Ground Shipping

Ground shipping is often an economical and efficient option for domestic distribution. Using trucks or trains, this service reaches various locations, from urban to rural areas.

Its ability to access many areas within one country makes it a favorite choice for many businesses, especially those that require regular deliveries in significant volumes.

2. Sea Shipping

Sea shipping is an ideal option for items in large quantities or those that require international shipping. The use of cargo ships or containers allows large-scale shipping from one port to another throughout the world.

Even though shipping times are longer compared to air, sea shipping is often chosen due to its lower costs for large volumes.

3. Air Freight

When speed is the top priority, air shipping is the best choice. Airplanes make it possible to send goods in a short time, both within the country and between countries.

This is especially useful for items that require immediate delivery such as electronic products, medicines, or other consumer goods.

4. Instant Delivery

Instant delivery services offer a very fast solution for shipping goods. Usually, the time between sending and receiving goods is only a matter of hours.

This is suitable for urgent needs such as important documents, urgent spare parts, or products that require quick handling.

5. Sameday Delivery

Sameday delivery guarantees that the goods will arrive at their destination on the same day they are sent.

This provides flexibility for shippers to ensure consumer needs are met in a short time.

6. Delivery by Trucking Cooler

This delivery service uses trucks with a refrigeration system to maintain a certain temperature during delivery.

This is crucial for products that require controlled temperatures such as frozen foods, pharmaceutical products, or certain chemicals.

7. Delivery with Dry Trucking

On the other hand, dry trucking services are suitable for shipping goods that do not require controlled temperatures.

Products such as clothing, household appliances, or non-perishable goods can be sent with this service.

8. Towing Services

This service is specifically for shipping vehicles, such as cars or other heavy vehicles.

By using special towing vehicles, this service helps in moving vehicles from one location to another.

9. International Shipping

International shipping not only involves transporting goods between countries, but also dealing with customs aspects, import and export regulations, as well as legal differences that apply in the destination country.

10. City Courier

In-city courier service that allows goods to be delivered quickly and on time within the city environment. This is very helpful in meeting the needs of local consumers who expect delivery in a short time.
